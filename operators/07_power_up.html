💡 The Exponent Operator

In Python, raising a number to a power (exponentiation) is written with two asterisks **. It multiplies the base number by itself, the number of times specified by the exponent. For example, 3 ** 2 means 3 * 3, which equals 9.

Score (Base): 3
Power-Up Level: 2
3 ** 2 = 9
